Subspace Dimension.

In many cases it is useful to choose p the dimension of the subspace larger than n_{\mathrm{ev}} (the number of eigenvalues sought). In that case the slowest eigenvalue converges at a rate \vert\lambda_{p+1}/\lambda_{n_{\mathrm{ev}}}\vert which may be significantly smaller than \vert\lambda_{n_{\mathrm{ev}}+1}/\lambda_{n_{\mathrm{ev}}}\vert. Practitioners in structural engineering have used choices like p = \mathrm{min} (2n_{\mathrm{ev}},n_{\mathrm{ev}}+8); see [42].
