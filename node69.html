Equivalences.

Suppose X and Y are nonsingular matrices. Let \hat{A}= Y^*AX and \hat{B}= Y^*BX. We say that the pencil \hat{A}- \lambda \hat{B} is equivalent to A - \lambda B and that X and Y are equivalence transformations. \hat{A}- \lambda \hat{B} and A - \lambda B have the same eigenvalues. If \mathcal X (\mathcal Y) is a right (left) reducing subspace of A - \lambda B, then \hat{\mathcal X}=X^{-1} {\mathcal X} ( \hat{\mathcal Y} = Y^{*}{\mathcal Y}) is a right (left) reducing subspace of \hat{A}- \lambda \hat{B}.
