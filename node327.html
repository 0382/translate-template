Error Bound for Computed Eigenvalues.

It can be proved [425] that up to the first order of \Vert(E,F)\Vert _2, there is an eigenvalue (\alpha,\beta) of A and B satisfying
\chi((\alpha, \beta),(\widetilde\alpha,\widetilde\beta))\simle c_{(\alpha,\beta)} \cdot \Vert(E_2,F_2)\Vert _2,
 (244)

where

c_{(\alpha,\beta)}\equiv\frac {\Vert x\Vert _2\Vert y\Vert......\sqrt {\vert y^{\ast} Ax\vert^2 + \vert y^{\ast} Bx\vert^2}}.

It is called the individual condition number of (\alpha,\beta), which in actual computations will be estimated with the exact eigenvectors x and y replaced by their approximations \widetilde x and \widetilde y.
