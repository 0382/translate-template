Eigenvalues Sought.

We give three typical cases:
``IE''
stands for one or a few isolated eigenvalues at the end of the spectrum. This is the simplest case, a typical example being the computation of a few leading singular values of a data matrix.
``CE''
stands for one or several eigenvalues at the end of the spectrum, possibly clustered, a typical example being the computation of the lowest eigenmodes of a vibrating mechanical system, modeled by the finite element method.
``M,''
finally, means some eigenvalues in the middle of the spectrum, most often in a specified interval, like seeking all eigenmodes of a mechanical or electrical system excited by an external load of a prescribed frequency.
