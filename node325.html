Residual Vectors.

Let (\widetilde\alpha,\widetilde\beta) denote a computed eigenvalue, and let \widetilde x be its corresponding computed eigenvector, i.e.,

\widetilde\beta A \widetilde x\approx \widetilde\alpha B\widetilde x.

Sometimes, its corresponding left computed eigenvector \widetilde y is also available:

\widetilde\beta \widetilde y^{\ast} A\approx \widetilde\alpha\widetilde y^{\ast} B.

The residual vectors corresponding to the computed eigenvalue (\widetilde\alpha,\widetilde\beta) are defined as

r = \widetilde\beta A \widetilde x - \widetilde\alpha B\widetilde x \quad \mathrm{and}......ast} = \widetilde\beta \widetilde y^{\ast} A - \widetilde\alpha\widetilde y^{\ast} B,

respectively. For the simplicity, we normalize the approximate eigenvectors so that \Vert\widetilde x\Vert _2 = 1 and \Vert\widetilde y\Vert _2 = 1 and normalize the approximate eigenvalue so that \vert\widetilde\alpha\vert^2+\vert\widetilde\beta\vert^2=1.
